r1.0	"<Bold>r1.0 Introduction</Bold>
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
<Bold>PATTON'S BEST</Bold> recreates the World War II campaing in nothern Europe through the eyes of a tank commander in George Patton's most famous unit, the 4th Armored Division. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Patton's Best is designed as a solitaire game, with you acting as the commander of a Sherman tank. As the commander, you control the actions of your crew, and through them,
 fight and maneuver your tank. The actions of the enemy tanks and troops, as well as friendly forces  on your side, are randomly generated by various game aides and tables. 
Each scenario consists of a single engagement or a day  of movement and battle. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
However, the game realizes its full interest when played as a campaign which allows you to take part in every historical battle of the 4th Armored Division. 
You and your crew can witness and participate in the great drives across Europe, the battles for the Siegfried Line, the relief of Bastogne, and the crossing of the Rhine. Further details here:

r3.0	"<Bold>r3.0 Game Tables</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>Tables can be viewed by selecting the <Bold>Help | Show Tables...</Bold> menu.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>The game tables are used by rolling one or two ten-sided dice and reading the results generated by the table. 
Throughout the game, the notation 1D  means roll one die and 2D means rolling two die. The notation 1D/2 means roll one die and dividing the number by half.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>When rolled together, the colored die is the tens digit and the white die is the ones digit. A roll of '00' represents 100. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>Many tables include additioinal factors that modify the randomly generated number of the dice roll. For example, when rolling on the 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='Gun Malfunction' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> Repair Table, 
if the repairing crewman has a rating of 5, the roll modifier to repair is -5. "

r5.11	"<Bold>r5.11 Battle Area</Bold>
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The Battle Area is a distorted circle centered on a hexagonal hub. The counter representing your Sherman tank is placed in this hub and remains there throughout play, changing only the direction it is facing. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Radiating out from the hub are three rings, each corresponding to a range of distance: close range (out to 250 yards), medium range (out to 1000 yards), and long range (out to a mile). When enemy units are generated, they will be placed in the ring corresponding to the specified range. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The radiating rings are further divided into six sectors, each sector boundary originating at a corner of the hexagonal hub. Each sector thus has three zones, one for each range."
r5.12	"<Bold>r5.12 Sectors</Bold>
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The sectors are numbered at the bottom right: 1, 2, 3, 4-5 (1), 6-8 (2), and 9-10 (3). When an enemy unit is activated, roll 1D to determine which sector it appears. For a roll of 4+, put the unit in the sector corresponding to that number.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
For a roll of 1-3, the enemy unit appears only in the corresponding sector if no US Control marker is in the sector. If a US Control marker is present, then the unit appears in the sector where the number is shown in paratheses. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
For example, if a 2 was rolled and Sector 2 was marked US Control, the unit would be placed in Sectors 6-8 instead. Conversely, if a 6 was rolled and Sector 6-8 is marked as US Control but Sector 2 is not, the unit would appear in Sector 2. If both paired sectors are US Controlled, the enemy unit does not appear.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The US Control markers represent the presence of friendly forces in your support. After an engagement begins, the number of US Controlled sectors may increase or decrease due to actions from your tank, Enemy Actions 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='r13.0' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>, or Random Events 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='r15.0' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>."

r6.1	"<Bold>r6.1 Victory Points</Bold>
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Victory points are scored for knocking out enemy units and capturing enemy territory. They are subtracted for loss of friendly forces and the loss of US controlled territory.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The points scored for each acheivement are shown on the After Action Report 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='AAR' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>.
 Each time a victory point action occurs, the value is added on the AAR. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
When the scenario ends, total the victory points into four categories:<LineBreak/>
-- Enemy units KO'd by your tank<LineBreak/>
-- Enemy units KO'd by friendly forces<LineBreak/>
-- Territory controlled or lost<LineBreak/>
-- Friendly force losses (tanks and infantry squads)"
r6.2	"<Bold>r6.2 Victory</Bold>
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
When the engagement or scenario ends, multiply the victory points in the four categories by the multipliers shown on the After Action Report 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='AAR' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Depending on whether the 4th Armored Division is advancing, engaged in a pitched battle, or defending against a counterattack; the gain or loss of territory and the destruction of enemy units will be more or less important.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
If the sum of the adjusted positive and negative points is positive, you win. If the sum is negative, you lose."

r9.0	"<Bold>r9.0 Sherman Main Gun Fire</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The American versions of the Sherman tank available for play are armed with one of two types of main gun: either a 75mm or 76mm gun. The &quot;L&quot; denotes improved long range capability. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The Main gun is directed to fire by placing either a Fire Main Gun or Ratate and Fire marker in the gunner&apos;s box on the Tank Card. The gun cannot be fired if it is not loaded or is malfunctioning. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Resolution of a shot from the main gun requires five steps: 1.) Selecting eligible target 2.) Selecting Direct Fire or Area Fire 3.) Hitting the target 4.) For vehicle targets only, determining where the target was hit, and 5.) Determining if the target is destroyed. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
However, you do not see the results of any hits until you have finished firing.

r9.1	"<Bold>r9.1 Selecting a Target</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>

<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='r17.0' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> may be fired on. 
The main gun may only fire at a target in the sector to the turret&apos;s front. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>

<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='To Hit Target' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> Table.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The gun may be fired at targets at any range. The effectiveness of fire drops at longer ranges. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The main gun may be fired at only one unit per battle round, regardless of how many Rate of Fire shots are taken. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Infantry targets are LW, MG, and AT guns. All other enemy units are vehicles."
r9.2	"<Bold>r9.2 Direct or Area Fire</Bold>

Direct Fire represents aiming a shot at a specific target. Area Fire represents shooting into a general area. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
All AP and HVAP shots must use Direct Fire or they have no effect. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
WP and HCBI shots must use Area Fire.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
HE ammo can use either Direct or Area Fire."

r9.35	"<Bold>r9.35 Target Acquisition</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
After your first shot at a target, place an &quot;Acquired Target 1&quot; marker on it. Place an &quot;Acquired Target 2&quot; marker on a target that already has one if you shoot a second time. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Acquired target modifiers to the To Hit dice rolls apply only to targets marked with the acquired markers. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Only one target at a time may be acquired by your tank. Acquired markers are removed if your tank moves or pivots unless your crew has a gyrostablizer per 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='r27.2' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>

<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>

-- The normal load for a Sherman tank included six smoke grenades and 14 smoke bombs for the 2"" smoke mortar.<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
-- In earlier models, ammo was stored in sponson boxes in the hull sides. In later models, ammo was stored in the floor and between the drivers in boxes flooded with a solution of glycol to retard fires (i.e., wet stowage).<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
-- Extra main gun and MG ammo could be stuffed into the tank if desired, but at the cost of increased crew discomfort and danger. See

r16.1	"<Bold>r16.1 Ammo Types</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
-- <Bold>Armor Piercing (AP):</Bold> AP rounds were designed to maximize muzzle velocity to achieve penetration of enemy armor vehicles and then explode inside.<LineBreak/>
-- <Bold>High Explosives (HE):</Bold> HE rounds maximized the amount of explosive contained to destroy and neutralize unarmored targets with blast and shrapnel.<LineBreak/>
-- <Bold>White Phosphorous (WP):</Bold> WP (nick-named willy peter) ignited phosphorous upon detonation. They were used to put smoke on an enemy position to restrict their ability to see.<LineBreak/>
-- <Bold>Hexachlorothane Base Initiating (HBCI):</Bold> HCBI rounds were chemical smoke used to obsure friendly positions where the fire of WP rounds would be dangerous.<LineBreak/>
-- <Bold>High Velocity Armor AP (HVAP):</Bold> AHVAP rounds were a special &quot;hot&quot; round with a rigid core and increased muzzle velocity. 
They were offically issued only to tank destroyer units, but all 76 gun crews scrounged a couple for use in an emergency against German tanks."

r17.11	"<Bold>r17.11 Spotting Roll</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
For each crewman attempting to spot a enemy unit, roll 1D. If the roll is less than or equal to the crewman's rating, he has spotted the unit. Mark the unit with a Spotted marker.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
                                     <InlineUIContainer><Image Name='c32Spotted'  Height='100' Width='100'></Image></InlineUIContainer>  "
r17.12	"<Bold>r17.12 Spotting Results</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
If the roll is equal to half or less than half of the crewman's rating (rounded down), he has also identified the unit. Consult the 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='Enemy Appearance' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> 
Table to detemine the specifc type of previously unidentified enemy tanks, SPs, and AT guns."
r17.13	"<Bold>r17.13 Spotting Modifiers</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
There are a number of factors that can modify the spotting die roll as listed in the 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='Spotting' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> 
Table. Small, large, and very large targets are identifed with S, L, and VL on the counters."
r17.14	"<Bold>r17.14 Automatic Spotting Failure</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
A spotting attempt roll of 9 or 10 before any modifications always means the unit is hidden from view in its current position and spotting is impossible by any other crewman this round. Mark any hidden unit with a Hidden Marker.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
You may not attempt to spot a hidden unit. A spotted unit cannot become hidden unless either it or your tank moves in following rounds.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
                                     <InlineUIContainer><Image Name='c31Hidden'  Height='100' Width='100'></Image></InlineUIContainer>  "
r17.15	"<Bold>r17.15 Spotting Continuation</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
An enemy unit that is neither spotted nor hidden remains unspotted and can be looked  for next round. Unspotted units have no marker."

<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The sectors in which each crewman may attempt to spot are noted on the Tank Card 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='r2.13' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>. 
Each crewman may attempt to spot every unit in the sector he can see. For example, the commander can spot into all sectors on an open hatch and thus may attempt to spot every enemy unit on the Battle Board."
r17.22	"<Bold>r17.22 Crew Action Restrictions</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Performing certain crew actions may prevent a crewman from attempting to spot. Crew actions that allow spotting to continue have a spotting symbole on their marker.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
A crewman is considred to be performing an action until the next opportunity to change the Crew Action marker. For example, if the loader was ordered to repair the co-axial MG and has done so, he is assumed to be involved in that action throught the Spotting Phase and cannot attempt to spot."
r17.23	"<Bold>r17.23 Hatches Restrictions</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Crewmen with closed hatches (other than the gunner) that have broken periscopes may not spot. The gunner may spot if either his sight or periscope is unbroken."
r17.24	"<Bold>r17.24 Weather Restrictions</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
During falling snow and fog weather, spotting is only possible at close range."
r17.25	"<Bold>r17.25 Smoke Restrictions</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Spotting rolls are +1 for each Smoke marker on your tank, in the zone with the unit you are attempting to spot, or in any zones between."

r17.42	"<Bold>r17.42 Unidentified Example</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>

Unidentified units are treated as the unit pictured for all purposes until identified. It is critical to identify counters as quickly as possible. Continue attempting to spot unidentified to spot unidentified units until they are identified."

r18.31	"<Bold>r18.31 On Fire Attacks</Bold>
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
All fire into, out of, and through a zone containing smoke has a reduced chance of hitting its target. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
Each Smoke marker encountered reduces the base number needed 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='To Hit Target' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> 
Table by one half (round down). 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
For example, if employing Direct Fire at medium range infantry target with a 76L gun (base to hit number of 45), and there are two Smoke markers in the target&apos;s zone and one in the close range zone, the to hit number is reduced to 05. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
( [0.5 x 0.5 x 0.5 = 1/8] ) x 45 = 5.6 rounded down to 5"

When playing, it is possible for the crewmen of your tank to be wounded or killed. Crew casualties can occur when you tank is knocked out

When an enemy attack successfully rolls To Kill your tank, it has been knocked out and your must check crew casualties.

<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='Bail Out' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> Table; then roll again on the 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='Wounds' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> Table for each crewman who gets out of the tank."
r19.14	"<Bold>r19.14 Crew Rescue</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>Crewmen who have escaped from a knocked out tank and are not seriously wounded may attempt to rescue other crew members 
who failed to bail out. Each crewman on the outside may attempt one rescue and only one rescue may be attempted for each crewman inside. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>For each rescue attempt, roll once again on the 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='Wounds' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> Tablefor the rescuer. 
If the rescurer is not seriously wounded or killed during the rescue attempt, he automatically frees the trapped man. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>Do not roll for additional wounds for the trapped man. If the rescuer is killed or seriously wounded while attempting the rescue, 
the man inside is trapped."
r19.2	"<Bold>r19.2 With Open Hatches</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>Crewmen with open hatches are considered to be partially exposed outside the tank and may become casualties when either Enemy Action 
or Random Events call for a check on the <InlineUIContainer><Button Content='Collateral' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> Damage Table. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
This represents the danger of being exposed to random artillery and small arms fire. When called for, consult the Collateral Damage Table to see which, if any, crew positions are threatened. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
If that crew member has an open hatch, roll on the 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='Wounds' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>
 Table to determine if he is wounded. Wounds can either be non-serious 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='r19.21' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> or 
serious <InlineUIContainer><Button Content='r19.22' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer> wounds."
r19.21	"<Bold>r19.21 Non-Serious Wounds</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
As long as a crew member does not take a wound that puts him out of action for a week or more, he may continue to function normally."
r19.22	"<Bold>r19.22 Serious Wounds</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
If a crew member takes a wound that puts him out of action for one week or more, he is incapacitated and immediately ceases performing any crew actions. 
Note incapacitated crewmen in the notes section of the After Action Report (AAR) 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='AAR' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
The assistant driver may move through the tank to replace any one incapacitated crewmen. An incapacitated driver is moved to the assistant driver's position, and incapacitated men in the turret are placed on the turret floor.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
While occupying any other position except driver, the assistant driver's rating is halved (rounded down). This change takes one full round."
r19.3	"<Bold>r19.3 Replacing Crew Casualties</Bold> 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
A tank with one or more incapacitated crew members must continue to function to the best of its remaining ability until either the battle ends, the tank is knocked out, or the tank is able to retreat out of the area 
<InlineUIContainer><Button Content='r11.3' FontFamily='Courier New'  FontSize='12'></Button></InlineUIContainer>.
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
If the tank is knocked out, the day ends for the tank and replacement crewmen are rolled for at the beginning of the next day of combat. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
If the tank withdraws or the battle ends, replace incapacitated crewmen immediately by rolling for a new man. Then continue the scenario.

r27.2	"<Bold>r27.2 Gyrostabilizer</Bold>
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
A gyrostabilizer was attached to the main gun of the Sherman tank is to keep the gun stabilized, or steady, in the vertical axis (up-down). 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
When turned on, it would keep the gun in a level position while the tank moved.  In theory, this allowed the gunner to keep the gun aimed at a target when moving by adjusting only the turret&apos;s rotation. 
<LineBreak/><LineBreak/>
In practice, most crews found the Stabilizer dangerous (the gun would move up and down on its own) and difficult to use. However, others thought the Stabilizer was a great asset if properly used.
